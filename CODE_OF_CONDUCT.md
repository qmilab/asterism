# Code of Conduct

Asterism is a small project that wants to stay a good place to work. The whole
idea here is keeping separate things separate and treating each one with care —
the same applies to people.

## The short version

- **Be kind and assume good faith.** Most disagreements are about the work, not
  the person. Keep it that way.
- **Be direct and be respectful.** You can be exacting about code and warm to
  the human who wrote it. Critique the change, not the contributor.
- **Make room.** Newcomers, beginners, and people from every background are
  welcome. A patient answer is worth more than a clever one.
- **No harassment, discrimination, or personal attacks.** This includes
  demeaning language, unwelcome attention, and any conduct that would make a
  reasonable person feel unsafe or unwelcome.

This applies everywhere the project lives — issues, pull requests, discussions,
and any other space that represents Asterism.

## If something goes wrong

If you experience or witness behavior that breaks this code, please report it
**privately** to the maintainers. The most reliable private channel is GitHub's
[private vulnerability / report flow](https://github.com/qmilab/asterism/security)
or a direct message via the maintainer's GitHub profile. Reports are handled
discreetly and in good faith.

Maintainers may remove comments, commits, issues, and PRs that violate this
code, and may block repeat or serious offenders from participating.
